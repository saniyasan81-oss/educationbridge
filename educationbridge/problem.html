The Education Crisis

Understanding the scale and depth of the global learning divide

258 Million Children & Youth Are Out of School

According to UNESCO, 258 million children, adolescents, and youth between the ages of 6 and 17 are not receiving any form of education. That's 1 in every 6 children worldwide.

Even among those who attend school, learning outcomes remain critically low. In low-income countries, nearly 90% of children cannot read and understand a simple text by age 10.

Key Factors Driving the Gap

Economic Barriers

School fees, uniforms, and supplies force families to choose between education and survival.

Gender Inequality

Girls are disproportionately denied education, especially in regions with cultural barriers.

Geographic Isolation

Rural communities often have no schools within safe walking distance.

Disability Exclusion

Children with disabilities are 49% more likely to never attend school.

Conflict & Displacement

War and instability destroy school infrastructure and displace millions of students.

Language Barriers

Instruction in non-native languages leads to high dropout rates.
